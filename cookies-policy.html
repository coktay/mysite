Cookie Policy

How we use cookies on ShareToSave

Last Updated: 13 August 2025

This Cookie Policy explains what cookies are, how I use them on sharetosave.co.uk, and how you can control them.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device when you visit a website. They help websites function properly, improve your experience, and collect information about how you use the site.

2. How I Use Cookies

I use cookies for the following purposes:

Essential cookies – Necessary for the website to work correctly (e.g., security, load balancing).

Performance and analytics cookies – To understand how visitors use the blog and improve content (e.g., Google Analytics or similar tools).

Affiliate tracking cookies – To track clicks on affiliate links so that partner companies can credit me if you sign up or make a purchase.

3. Third-Party Cookies

Some cookies are set by third-party services that appear on this blog, such as:

  • Affiliate programs (e.g., Octopus Energy) – for tracking referrals.
  • Analytics services – to monitor visitor numbers and behaviour.

These third parties have their own privacy and cookie policies, which you should review separately.

4. Managing Cookies

You can control and delete cookies through your browser settings. Please note:

  • Disabling essential cookies may affect how the website functions.
  • You can opt out of analytics cookies without affecting your ability to use the blog.

For more guidance on managing cookies, visit:
https://www.aboutcookies.org

5. Consent

When you first visit this blog, you'll see a cookie banner that lets you accept or reject non-essential cookies. By continuing to use the site after making your choice, you agree to this Cookie Policy.

6. Changes to This Policy

I may update this Cookie Policy from time to time. Any changes will be posted on this page with a new "Last Updated" date.

7. Contact Me

If you have any questions about this Cookie Policy, please use the contact form on this website.
